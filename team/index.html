Mary Beth Stern, Certified MBSR Teacher

Mary Beth Stern is a Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) teacher, trained and certified by the Center for Mindfulness at UMASS Medical School. She is a Florida Board Certified Addictions counselor, and a qualified facilitator in the app-based Habit Change Programs including Eat Right Now and Unwinding Anxiety, developed by Judson Brewer, MD. She continues advanced mindfulness training at the Center for Mindfulness at Brown University. 

She is Level 2 MBITAC ( Mindfulness- Based Intervention Teacher Assessment Criteria) trained and actively participates in several peer mentoring groups. 

Since 2014, she has taught throughout south Florida, offering mindfulness training at hospitals, behavioral health facilities and substance abuse treatment centers. She is currently a senior teacher for Mindful Leader, offering MBSR online and individual mindfulness coaching to an international global population.​

Dr. Vanessa Miller-Curtis

Vanessa Miller-Curtis, Ph.D. has over 12 years of experience in a variety of settings. Vanessa has spent several years developing and leading educational programs in the areas of Mindfulness, Shame and Guilt, and Contemplative Psychology in prison settings, outpatient clinics, and brain injury recovery and support groups.

The effectiveness of therapeutic and educational programming for anyone on their overall well-being and its use as a tool to address anger, anxiety and other mental health issues is at the core of this work. Therapeutic educational programs pertain to psychological health and well-being. The importance of therapeutic and educational programming for any human being cannot adequately be expressed. For some, this may be the first opportunity to address a lifetime of maladaptive behaviors. The changes in thoughts, emotions, and behavior as a result of continued exposure to therapeutic and educational programming often creates an intangible shift in attitudes and values.

Vanessa has a strong daily Mindfulness and MBSR personal practice. She says, “engaging in Mindfulness and MBSR daily has helped shift my perspective and provide a balance in life like no other program has been able to do.” Vanessa holds a Ph.D. in Cognitive Psychology from the University of California, Riverside and a Master’s degree in Clinical Psychology from Chapman University.

Leila Nabors, Certified MBSR Teacher, Co-Founder

Leila Nabors, M.Ed., LPC has been in private practice as a psychotherapist for over 18 years. She has over 25 years of experience as a counselor in private practice, community mental health and public schools.  In private practice Leila became interested in Mindfulness-Based Interventions (MBI) as a therapeutic approach and Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) as a psychoeducational program to enhance healing and increase well-being.

Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction is a  program that uses mindfulness and meditation to help people cope more effectively with stress and illness by increasing present moment awareness.   Leila has studied with Dr. Jon Kabat-Zinn who developed Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) in the late 1970’s at the University of Massachusetts Medical School.  After six years of training and practice, she received certification as a Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction teacher.  

Leila is the only certified teacher in the state of Alabama and one of only a handful in the Southeast. She and Beth Cook founded the Alabama Institute for Mindfulness in 2015. 

Beth Cook, LPC, Co-Founder

Beth Cook, MS, LPC, is a psychotherapist with 30 years of experience in a variety of settings including community mental health centers, public schools, inpatient psychiatric facilities and, since 2005, private practice. In addition to a foundation in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy she has trained extensively in the areas of spiritually-oriented and mind/body therapeutic models. Throughout her career, Beth has been interested in the intersection of emotional, physical and spiritual aspects of health and wholeness and she now integrates mindfulness practice into the psychotherapeutic interventions she uses with clients.

Beth has completed the 8-week Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction class and maintains a regular meditation practice. She is also a certified spiritual director who honors the wisdom found in all of the world’s enduring spiritual and religious traditions.

Beth is a core faculty member of the Metagem Institute which offers training in spiritual direction and the contemplative path. In this role she teaches mindfulness as an integrative practice – one which contributes to the practitioner’s own self-knowledge and well-being. Beth and Leila founded the Alabama Institute for Mindfulness in 2015.
